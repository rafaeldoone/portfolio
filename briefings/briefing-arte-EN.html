Question 02
What surface or material will the artwork be applied to?
Paper, fabric, wall, ceramic, and wood all have completely different technical requirements. This defines the technique and the pricing.
Question 03
What's the expected size or dimension?
Size directly impacts execution time and pricing. An A4 lettering piece and a 10m² mural are completely different projects.
Question 04
Will the artwork include any specific text, phrase, or word?
Lettering and custom typography have a different creative process than pure illustration. Knowing the text content from the start is essential.
Question 05
Does the artwork need to follow your existing brand identity?
Pieces that need to match an existing identity have different creative constraints than open projects. I need to know what's fixed before I start.
02

CONTEXT

Question 06
What's the purpose of the piece? (select all that apply)
Artwork made for product printing has very different technical requirements than something made for social media or a physical space.
Personal gift or commission Commercial use Interior decoration Event or exhibition Social media Product for sale Other
Question 07
Will the artwork be reproduced or is it a one-of-a-kind piece?
This affects licensing, exclusivity, and pricing. Artwork that becomes a product print has a different value than a unique decorative piece.
Question 08
Who is this piece for? Who will see or use it?
Understanding the audience helps calibrate the visual tone — something for a personal gift feels different from a piece for an exhibition or a brand product.
03

REFERENCES

Question 09
Do you have any visual references for this piece?
Concrete references save time and avoid rework. It doesn't have to be something you want copied — it could be a color palette, a style, a mood.
Question 10
What feeling or atmosphere should the piece convey?
Even a simple piece communicates something. Knowing the emotional intention before starting avoids deliveries that are technically correct but feel wrong.
Question 11
Do you have any color preferences or things you'd rather avoid?
Colors carry emotional memory. Even if the palette gets defined in the process, knowing what you love and hate avoids paths that will need to be redone.
Question 12
Is there anything you absolutely DON'T want in this piece?
Knowing what to avoid is just as important as knowing what to aim for. Styles, references, or elements you hate are valuable data.
04

DELIVERY

Question 13
What file format do you need?
Printing, embroidery, laser cutting, and digital use all have different technical formats. Knowing this upfront avoids rework and incompatible files.
High-res PNG Vector PDF AI (Adobe Illustrator) SVG Other
Question 14
Do you need exclusivity over the artwork?
By default, original artwork may be displayed in my portfolio. If you need full exclusivity — no sharing, no reuse — that's included in the contract and affects the price.
05

PROJECT

Question 15
Do you have a specific date or deadline to meet?
Urgency changes planning and may be factored into the proposal. If you don't have one, leave it blank.
Question 16
What's your budget for this project?
Transparency here saves time on both sides. No judgment — every budget range allows for a different scope, and it's better to align this before we start.
Question 17
How would you prefer to be contacted?
Simple as it sounds, this avoids reaching out through the wrong channel and leaving you wondering why you haven't heard back.
Question 18

If there's any detail that didn't quite fit the other questions, this is the place. A context, a technical constraint, an extra reference — anything you feel is relevant.
